Strengthening Fiscal Sustainability: Global Debt & Deficit Analysis

This policy brief and interactive Tableau dashboard analyze global trends in government spending, revenue, and public debt from 2010 to 2023. Using datasets from the IMF, World Bank, OECD, and other international sources, I identified widespread fiscal deficits and examined how persistent borrowing impacts long term debt sustainability.

I designed comparative visualizations including debt trend lines, cross country scatterplots, and debt growth bubble charts to translate complex macroeconomic data into accessible insights. The project culminates in evidence based policy recommendations focused on fiscal rules, revenue mobilization, and transparent debt management.

02

Impacts of Gun Characteristics on the Lethality of Gun Violence in the United States

This statistical analysis investigates how firearm characteristics influence casualty rates in U.S. gun violence incidents using a comprehensive 2017 dataset. I examined whether long barrel versus short barrel firearms and stolen versus non stolen status were associated with statistically significant differences in casualties per incident.

Through comparative bar charts and proportion analysis, we found that long guns and legally possessed firearms were associated with higher casualty proportions. My role focused on structuring the research questions, interpreting statistical results, and designing clear visualizations that made sensitive findings understandable without oversimplification.
